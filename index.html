8 threat types monitored

We watch for what attackers
actually look for.

Sentinel monitors your network continuously for the attack patterns that most frequently hit business infrastructure, the ones that rarely get caught until it is too late.

Unauthorized devices

Any device that connects to your network without authorization is flagged immediately. Rogue laptops, unknown phones, or IoT devices that do not belong there.

Network reconnaissance

Attackers probe networks before breaking in. We detect port scanning and mapping activity before they find a way inside your systems.

Brute force attacks

Hundreds of rapid login attempts against your routers, servers, or employee accounts are caught and flagged in real time before access is gained.

Malicious connections

DNS requests to known malware distribution servers, command and control infrastructure, and data exfiltration endpoints are identified and blocked.

Man in the middle

ARP spoofing and traffic interception attempts where attackers reroute your data through their devices are detected before any information is captured.

Data exfiltration

Unusual outbound data volumes, a sign that customer records, financial files, or employee data may be quietly leaving your network, are caught early.

Ransomware activity

Rapid file encryption patterns and suspicious mass write behavior are detected early, before ransomware can lock your systems and demand payment.

Phishing & credentials

Connections to known phishing domains and credential harvesting sites are blocked before any employee account information can be captured.

Continuously updated

Our detection rules are tuned weekly against fresh threat intelligence, so the patterns we watch for tomorrow reflect what is happening on real networks today.

This is what you would actually receive.

Every alert is written for a business owner, not a network engineer. Clear context, clear instructions, no technical background required.

Critical Detected 12 seconds ago Main Office Network

Unauthorized Device Connected to Your Network

What happened

A device we do not recognize just joined your office Wi‑Fi. Its hardware address does not match any of your registered equipment, and it has already attempted to communicate with three other devices on your network.

What to do right now
  1. Open your router admin panel at 192.168.1.1
  2. Go to Connected Devices or Device List
  3. Find the entry labelled "unknown 38:f9:d3"
  4. Select Block Device to remove it immediately
  5. Change your Wi‑Fi password under Wireless Settings
